# Skill: Post Writer

> Write a complete Instagram caption in Mike's voice — hook, story, and CTA — ready to format and post.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

This is the core content skill. Claude writes full Instagram captions from a brief — dish, story angle, mood, and CTA preference. The caption should feel like Mike wrote it: sensory, confident, story-first, with the recipe as a reveal at the end.

---

## Instructions

1. **Get the brief:**
   - Dish or subject
   - Story angle or inspiration (market find, memory, Sunday dinner, technique obsession, season)
   - Mood (nostalgic, punchy, seductive, funny, contemplative)
   - CTA preference (comment for recipe / save / Substack)
   - Any specific lines or details Mike wants included
2. **Write the caption in this structure:**
   - **Line 1:** Hook — one sentence, stops the scroll, survives truncation
   - **Body:** 3–6 short paragraphs — story builds toward the dish, not away from it
   - **Recipe tease:** one line that hints at the recipe without giving it away
   - **CTA:** one line, standalone, Mike's standard moves only
3. **Voice rules (non-negotiable):**
   - Sensory and poetic — make it smell, taste, feel
   - Story-driven — connected to a moment, place, memory, or season
   - Vibe arrives first, recipe drops last
   - No food blogger language (see Voice Profile Section 7)
   - No exclamation points unless Mike specifically requests
   - No emojis unless Mike specifically requests
4. **Write 2 versions** — one slightly longer (full story), one tighter (punchy). Mike picks.

## Calibration Check

Before finalizing, run the caption against this test:
- Does the hook work as a standalone sentence?
- Is there a real story or just description?
- Does it sound like Mike or like a food blogger?
- Is there exactly one CTA?

---

## Example Prompt

> *"Write me an Instagram caption for [dish]. Story angle: [inspiration]. Mood: [mood]. CTA: [CTA type]. Give me two versions — full story and punchy."*
