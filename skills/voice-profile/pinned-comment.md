# Skill: Pinned Comment

> Write the pinned comment that goes under every IG post — drives saves, Substack clicks, and engagement.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

The pinned comment is the most underused real estate on Instagram. It's the first comment people see, it lives below the caption, and it's a second chance to drive action. Claude writes a pinned comment that extends the post — adds a detail, a tease, or a direct CTA — without repeating the caption.

---

## Instructions

1. **Get the post context:** caption summary, dish, CTA used in the caption, and what Mike wants the pinned comment to do (drive saves, drive Substack clicks, start a conversation, add a recipe detail).
2. **Write 2–3 pinned comment options** — each with a different angle:
   - **Recipe tease** — hint at an ingredient or technique without giving it away
   - **Substack bridge** — direct people to the newsletter with a specific reason to click
   - **Engagement starter** — ask a specific question that makes people want to answer
3. **Voice rules:**
   - Conversational, like Mike is talking directly to one person
   - Short — 1–3 sentences max
   - No hashtags in the pinned comment
   - Can use one emoji maximum if it fits naturally
4. **Never repeat the caption CTA verbatim** — the pinned comment should add something new.

---

## Output Format

**Option 1 — [angle]:**
[comment text]

**Option 2 — [angle]:**
[comment text]

**Option 3 — [angle]:**
[comment text]

---

## Examples of Strong Pinned Comments

> *"Full recipe is on Substack this week — including the one thing most people skip that makes the whole dish. Link in bio."*

> *"The secret is the fond. Don't rush it. That's all I'll say here."*

> *"Tell me — do you go butter or olive oil to finish? I'll wait."*

---

## Example Prompt

> *"Write me 3 pinned comment options for this post about [dish]. Caption CTA was [CTA used]. I want one that teases the recipe, one that drives Substack, and one that starts a conversation."*
