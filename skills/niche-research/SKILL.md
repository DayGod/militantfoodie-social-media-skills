# Skill: Niche Research

> Find out what's working right now in the food content space — trends, formats, hooks, and gaps Mike can own.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

Claude uses web search to research what's trending in food content, then filters it through Mike's voice and niche to identify what's worth acting on. The goal is not to chase trends — it's to find the intersection of *what's working* and *what Mike can do better than anyone else.*

---

## Instructions

1. **Search for current trends** across:
   - Food content on Instagram (formats, hooks, dish trends)
   - Substack food newsletters (topics gaining subscribers)
   - Seasonal ingredient or dish momentum
   - Emerging techniques or cuisines getting attention
   - Viral food moments or cultural food conversations
2. **Filter everything through Mike's lens:**
   - Does this fit his voice? (sensory, story-driven, confident)
   - Does this fit his themes? (Sunday dinners, markets, seasons, chef culture)
   - Is there a gap — is everyone doing it badly and Mike could do it well?
3. **Output three tiers:**
   - **Act on now** — high relevance, strong fit, timely
   - **Watch** — interesting but not quite right yet
   - **Skip** — trending but off-brand for Mike
4. **For each "Act on now" item:** suggest a specific post angle Mike could take.

---

## Output Format

### 🟢 Act On Now
**Trend:** [what's happening]
**Why it fits Mike:** [specific reason]
**Post angle:** [concrete idea in Mike's voice]

### 🟡 Watch
**Trend:** [what's happening]
**Why to watch:** [reason]

### 🔴 Skip
**Trend:** [what's happening]
**Why it's off-brand:** [reason]

---

## Example Prompt

> *"Research what's working in food content right now — Instagram and Substack. What trends fit my voice? What should I act on, what should I watch, and what should I skip? Give me specific post angles for anything worth doing."*
