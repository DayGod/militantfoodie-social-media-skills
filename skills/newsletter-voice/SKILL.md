# Skill: Newsletter Voice

> Write Substack newsletters in Mike's voice — personal, story-driven, worth reading every time.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

Mike's newsletter is not a recipe dump. It's a letter from someone who's been in the kitchen all week and has things to tell you. Claude writes full newsletter drafts — or sections of them — that feel personal, specific, and earned. The recipe is always there, but it lives inside a story.

---

## Instructions

1. **Get the brief:** dish, story angle, season, memory or inspiration, and whether Mike wants a full draft or just a specific section (opener, body, recipe intro, sign-off).
2. **Structure the newsletter:**
   - **Subject line:** specific, curiosity-driven, never clickbait — reads like a text from a friend
   - **Opening paragraph:** drop straight into the story — no preamble, no "hey everyone"
   - **Body:** 2–4 paragraphs of story, context, technique, or opinion — builds toward the recipe
   - **Recipe bridge:** one short paragraph that transitions into the recipe naturally
   - **Recipe:** headnote + ingredients + method (written in Mike's voice, not clinical)
   - **Sign-off:** brief, warm, personal — not a corporate farewell
3. **Voice rules:**
   - First person, past or present tense — never instructional second person in the story sections
   - Short paragraphs — 2–4 sentences max
   - One strong image per section — sensory detail that earns its place
   - Occasional dry humor — never forced
4. **Length:** 400–700 words for the story sections. Recipe is additional.

## Tone Calibration

The newsletter should read like this register:

> *"My grandmother never wrote down a single recipe. She cooked by feel, by memory, by the way things smelled when they were right. I spent three years trying to reverse-engineer her lasagna. This week, I got close enough."*

Personal. Direct. A little nostalgic. Always going somewhere.

---

## What to Avoid

- Opening with "Hey everyone" or "Welcome back"
- Bullet points in the story sections
- Explaining what the newsletter is about before just doing it
- Ending with "Let me know what you think in the comments!"
- Sounding like a recipe blog that added a paragraph at the top

---

## Example Prompt

> *"Write a Substack newsletter about [dish]. The story angle is [memory/inspiration/season]. Full draft — subject line, opener, body, recipe bridge, and sign-off. Mike's voice throughout."*
