# Skill: Post Formatter

> Format any draft caption for Instagram — spacing, line breaks, CTA placement, and hashtag strategy.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

A great caption can be ruined by bad formatting. Instagram truncates after 3 lines — everything after that is hidden behind "more." Claude formats Mike's captions so they work with the platform: the hook lands before the fold, the spacing is clean, and the hashtags are handled correctly.

---

## Instructions

1. **Take the raw caption draft** and format it for Instagram.
2. **Apply these formatting rules:**
   - **Line 1:** Hook — must be complete before any line break. No ellipsis. No "…more" cut-off.
   - **Line break after line 1** — single blank line to create visual separation
   - **Body:** 2–5 short paragraphs, 1–3 sentences each. One line break between each paragraph.
   - **CTA:** stands alone on its own line at the end, separated by a blank line
   - **Hashtags:** 5–10 max, placed after 5 dots on separate lines to push them below the fold:
     ```
     .
     .
     .
     .
     .
     #hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
     ```
3. **Hashtag selection:** mix of niche-specific (higher chance of ranking) and broad (reach). Always include: one dish-specific, one technique, one lifestyle, one location-optional, one evergreen food tag.
4. **Check voice:** read the formatted version back against the Voice Profile. Flag anything that sounds off.
5. **Output the formatted caption ready to copy-paste.**
