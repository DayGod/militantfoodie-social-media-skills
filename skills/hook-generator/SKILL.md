# Skill: Hook Generator

> Generate 5–10 scroll-stopping first lines for any post, reel, or newsletter — in Mike's voice.

---

## How Claude Uses This Skill

The hook is the most important line in any piece of content. Claude generates multiple variations across different hook styles so Mike can pick the one that feels right. Every hook must earn the scroll — it should create curiosity, desire, tension, or a strong point of view in one sentence.

---

## Instructions

1. **Get the subject:** dish, topic, story, or theme of the post.
2. **Generate hooks across these styles:**
   - **Curiosity gap** — hints at something without revealing it
   - **Bold statement** — confident, declarative, slightly provocative
   - **Sensory opener** — drops you into a smell, texture, or taste
   - **Nostalgic pull** — a memory, a place, a person
   - **Contradiction** — subverts expectations
   - **One-word + pause** — short, punchy, dramatic
   - **Time/occasion anchor** — roots it in a specific moment
3. **Write 5–10 hooks minimum.** More options = better selection.
4. **Voice check:** every hook must sound like Mike — not a food blogger, not a recipe site. Confident, sensory, occasionally funny.
5. **No emojis. No exclamation points. No "you're going to love this."**

---

## Output Format

List hooks numbered, with the style labeled in brackets:

1. *"The ragù took four hours. It was done in three."* [Curiosity gap]
2. *"Brown butter is just butter that decided to try."* [Bold statement]
3. *"It smelled like Sunday before I even started cooking."* [Sensory opener]

---

## Hook Killers — Never Write These

- "You won't believe how easy this is"
- "This recipe is a game changer"
- "I'm obsessed with this dish"
- Anything that starts with "So,"
- Anything that ends with "🔥"

---

## Example Prompt

> *"Give me 10 hooks for a post about [dish or topic]. Mix the styles — curiosity gap, sensory, bold statement, nostalgia. No food blogger energy."*
